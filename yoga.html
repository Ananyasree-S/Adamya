Yoga Postures

For a better mental health

Virabhadrasana

Referred to as “the warrior pose”, this asana requires steady breathing.Improves focus

Chakrasana

Known as the circle pose, this is a bit of a tricky asana but it helps gain calmness and focus.

Vajrasana

This is a meditative pose that is known to improve focus, calmness and digestion.

Garudasana

This pose requires you to breathe while holding your body in balance. Prevents anxiety attacks and improves focus.

Anjaneyasana

This pose is also known as “the high lunge”. Doing the crescent variation of this exercise is known to help improve your blood flow, calm your mind.

Shavasana

Known as “the dead body pose”, this asana is similar to lying down to take a nap thus calms you.

Balasana

Plays a pivotal role in stretching the entire body including the hip joint and muscles. Increases overall balance , stamina and blood circulation. And tones the muscles around the hip, ankles and thigh region

Uttanasana

This inverted posture is an extremely therapeutic posture that enhances your immunity, attenuates stress and relaxes the mind. Moreover, it also strengthens the calves, hamstrings and hips and reduces insomnia, fatigue and headache.

Sethubandhasana

the bridge pose is found highly significant in removing all the blockages from the mind and restoring peace and calmness. Extremely beneficial posture for relieving arthritic pain and strengthening the leg, thigh and back muscles

Virabhadrasana

While on two feet, spread your legs wide, turn your hips to one side, turn your ankles to the side you are facing. Bend the front leg to a 90-degree angle.Stretch your arms to your sides and breathe in deeply and evenly for 10 seconds.


Chakrasana

While standing, place your arms high above your head. Keeping your back straight, bend backwards.Do this slowly and till your palms reach the floor behind you.Keep your ankles firmly on the ground.


Vajrasana

Slowly, begin by sitting on your knees, stretch your ankles outward, bring your thighs and calves together. Then place your palms on your knees. Breathe in and out deeply for 30 seconds to a minute.


Garudasana

Breathe in deep, shift the weight of your body onto one leg.Slowly wrap one leg around the other. Sink your hips and gently bend your knees. Keep your ankle flat on the ground. Keep your breathing even and slow.Wrap one arm around the other at the elbows and wrists.


Anjaneyasana

While standing, lunge one leg forward.Bend the front knee, make sure your knee is in line with your ankle, stretch your back leg.Press your ankle flat against the ground on both ankles. Raise your arms to the sky and connect your palms.Breathe in deeply and breathe out in an even flow. Repeat 5 times per leg.


Shavasana

Lay flat on the ground, stretch your legs out.Stretch your arms beside you and let your palms face skyward. Close your eyes, maintain even breathing while your focus wanders around you. Listen to the sounds and continue breathing for 5 minutes.


Balasana

Get down on your knees, keeping the spine straight.Now slowly bend forwards such that both the thighs touch the chest. Keep bending forwards until your head is beyond the knee and touches the ground. Straighten both the arms backward on either side of your legs with your palms, downwards facing the floor. Be in this position for 20-25 seconds, take few seconds of break and again repeat 2-3 times.


Uttanasana

While standing, bring the feet hip-distance apart. Without bending your knees, slowly bend your body above the torso downwards. See that your knees are straight. You can allow your hands and hang down and rest your palms on the ground or just hold your feet to the ankles. Hold this position for 8-10 breaths, then slowly get back to the standing position.


Sethubandhasana

Start the posture by lying flat on your back. Now bend your knees and elbows. Place your feet flat on the floor close to the hips and your hands firmly on either side of the head. While supporting both your hands and legs on the ground, slowly lift your body up into the air. Hold this arching posture for 20-30 secs and slowly bring your body up into a standing pose.
